About

I am a data scientist with experience in data pipeline design, automation, analysis, and data visualization. My work focuses on developing efficient and transparent systems that transform complex datasets into accessible, decision-ready insights. I am particularly interested in how data visualization and analytics can enhance the accessibility and interpretability of complex information for diverse users and decision-making contexts.

Based in Rome, I work with the Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations (FAO), where I contribute to the design, analysis, and management of large-scale survey data workflows within the Data in Emergencies (DIEM) team. My approach combines technical precision with an emphasis on collaboration and practical application, ensuring that data effectively supports evidence-based decisions.
